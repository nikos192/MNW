The Forging Process

Material integrity before decoration.

Cold forging subjects a billet of 6061-T6 aluminium to extreme compressive force, aligning the grain structure of the metal to follow the part. The result is higher strength at lower mass than a comparable cast wheel.

Each MNW wheel begins as a precision-cut billet, then moves through press cycles and CNC machining until spoke geometry, pad height, bore, and final weight align with the target chassis.

No universal spacer logic. No generic offsets. The geometry is resolved around the vehicle rather than adjusted after the fact, then backed by passed JWL tests and stricter in-house impact standards.
